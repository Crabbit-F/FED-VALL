FED-VALL: FEDERATED NEURAL CODEC LANGUAGE MODEL VIA MIXTURE OF EXPERTS


Abstract

Federated Text-to-Speech (Fed-TTS) is a promising paradigm for institutions with audio training constraints, which avoids centralizing sensitive data, leading to significant cost reductions in corpus collection while ensuring data privacy. Despite the neural codec language model (VALL-E) has exceptional capabilities in text-to-speech tasks, its growing model parameters create a bottleneck in training speed due to high communication costs during synchronization. In this paper, we propose a communication-efficient federated neural codec language model (Fed-VALL) framework to address this issue. Our approach maintains a frozen model, transferring lightweight adapter modules between clients. To mitigate the diverse speaker conflicts, we leverage the mixture of experts (MOE). Experimental results show our framework reduces communication costs by over 97%, with comparable or superior performance to competitive baselines.
